Prep Time: 10 minutes | Cook Time: 30 minutes | Serves: 4-6

A comforting Filipino chicken soup with ginger, green papaya, and sayote â€“ warming, nutritious, and perfect for rainy days!

Ingredients

  • 1 kg chicken, cut into serving pieces (thighs and drumsticks work best)
  • 2 thumbs fresh ginger, sliced thinly
  • 1 medium onion, quartered
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 medium sayote (chayote squash), peeled and cut into wedges
  • 1 small green papaya, peeled, seeded, and cut into wedges (or use ripe papaya for sweetness)
  • 1 cup malunggay (moringa) leaves or spinach, washed
  • 1 tbsp fish sauce (patis)
  • 6-8 cups water or chicken broth
  • 2 tbsp cooking oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Optional: 2 green chilies (siling haba) for a mild kick

Instructions

  1. Heat the cooking oil in a large pot over medium heat. SautÃ© the garlic, ginger, and onion until fragrant and the ginger is lightly browned, about 2-3 minutes. This step infuses the oil with ginger's warming flavor.
  2. Add the chicken pieces to the pot. Stir to coat with the aromatics and cook until the chicken skin is lightly browned, about 5 minutes. Season with fish sauce, salt, and pepper.
  3. Pour in the water or chicken broth. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to a simmer. Skim off any scum that rises to the surface for a clearer broth. Cover and simmer for 15-20 minutes until the chicken is tender.
  4. Add the sayote and green papaya wedges. If using green chilies, add them now. Continue simmering for 8-10 minutes until the vegetables are just tender but still crisp.
  5. Stir in the malunggay leaves (or spinach) and cook for another 1-2 minutes until wilted. Do not overcook to retain nutrients and color. Taste and adjust seasoning if needed.
  6. Remove from heat and let it rest for a few minutes. Serve hot with steamed rice on the side.
